\begin{abstract}
Vision--Language--Action (VLA) policies built on 7B-parameter backbones are accurate but too
large for on-board robot inference. We present a comprehensive, closed-loop study of
post-training quantization for an OpenVLA-OFT policy on the LIBERO benchmark and distill it to
one actionable principle: \emph{quantization wants activation energy spread uniformly across
channels.} Concretely, (i) \textbf{weights are easy}---naive per-channel quantization is
near-lossless to 4 bits ($90.75\%$ vs.\ a $90.25\%$ bf16 baseline)---while
(ii) \textbf{activations are the wall}---naive 4-bit activation quantization collapses the
policy to $0\%$ because a few channels carry magnitudes up to $10^5\times$ the median.
(iii) A single orthogonal \emph{incoherence rotation} restores \textbf{W4A4} to
$88.8$--$90.5\%$ ($98$--$100\%$ retention) by cutting the median activation outlier ratio
from $18.0$ to $1.6$. We then ask \emph{which} rotation, and benchmark \textbf{16 transforms};
a single taxonomy---global dense mixers work, localized/permuting/energy-concentrating
transforms do not---explains every result, including the striking finding that \textbf{PCA is
actively harmful}. We introduce a \textbf{seconds-long proxy} that reproduces the closed-loop
ranking, identify \textbf{DCT as the deployment pick} (ties Hadamard with no power-of-two
fallback), map the reachable bit-width frontier (W4A4/W3A8 free, W3A4 the cliff, W2
unreachable), and give the exact compression ($2.7\times$ whole-model at W4A4, $4\times$ on
the quantized backbone). Code, harness, and leaderboard are released.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Vision--Language--Action models turn large vision--language backbones into robot policies
that map an image and a language instruction to low-level actions
\cite{openvla,openvlaoft}. Their accuracy rests on 7B-parameter backbones, but deploying such
models on embedded robot compute demands aggressive compression. Post-training quantization
(PTQ) is the natural tool---yet most PTQ research optimizes language-model perplexity or
open-loop token accuracy \cite{gptq,awq,smoothquant,quarot,spinquant}, not the
\emph{closed-loop} task success that matters for control, where per-step error compounds over
long horizons.

This paper is a single, end-to-end answer to three coupled questions: \emph{where} does
quantization hurt a VLA policy, \emph{what} is the cheapest fix, and \emph{how far} can one
push before accuracy breaks. Our contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Diagnosis.} On a closed-loop VLA benchmark, the quantization bottleneck is
        activation outliers, not weights (Sec.~\ref{sec:wall}), with a concrete mechanism:
        ``massive activation'' channels up to $10^5\times$ the median (Sec.~\ref{sec:mech}).
  \item \textbf{Fix.} A single orthogonal incoherence rotation enables \textbf{W4A4} at
        $98$--$100\%$ retention (Sec.~\ref{sec:wall}).
  \item \textbf{Benchmark.} A systematic comparison of \textbf{16 transforms} with a
        predictive taxonomy and a \textbf{seconds-long proxy} validated against closed-loop
        evaluation; DCT is the deployment pick (Sec.~\ref{sec:zoo}).
  \item \textbf{Deployment map.} The reachable bit-width frontier and exact model-size
        accounting (Sec.~\ref{sec:reach}--\ref{sec:size}), plus an open library.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background and Related Work}
\textbf{VLA policies.} OpenVLA \cite{openvla} and OpenVLA-OFT \cite{openvlaoft} couple a
vision encoder and a Llama-2-7B backbone with an action head; OFT regresses a continuous
action chunk from the backbone's last-layer hidden states, queried once per chunk.

\textbf{PTQ and outliers.} Weight-only PTQ (GPTQ \cite{gptq}, AWQ \cite{awq}) is mature;
activation quantization is bottlenecked by outlier channels. SmoothQuant \cite{smoothquant}
migrates scale from activations to weights; \emph{incoherence processing}
(QuaRot \cite{quarot}, SpinQuant \cite{spinquant}, QuIP \cite{quip}) inserts an orthogonal
rotation $Q$ so $Wx=(WQ^\top)(Qx)$ holds exactly while the rotated operands become
near-Gaussian. We bring these to closed-loop VLA control, isolate which transform matters,
and explain why.

\textbf{VLA quantization.} Concurrent VLA-specific PTQ \cite{qvla,quantvla} assumes diffusion
action heads and (at writing) released no code; our study is method-agnostic, closed-loop,
and reproducible.

\section{Method}
We quantize only the Llama backbone linears (the $84\%$ of parameters where the win is); the
vision tower, projector, lm\_head, embeddings, and regression head stay bf16. All experiments
use \emph{simulated} (fake) quantization---quantize then dequantize in floating point---so
that closed-loop success isolates the algorithm from kernel artifacts.

\paragraph{Quantizers.} Weights: symmetric per-output-channel,
$\hat w=\mathrm{round}(w/s)s$, $s=\max|w|/(2^{b-1}{-}1)$. Activations: symmetric per-token at
$a$ bits. A configuration is denoted W$b$A$a$.

\paragraph{Preconditioning framework.} For each backbone linear we insert an invertible
preconditioner $M$ on the input axis,
\begin{equation}
y = Wx = (WM^{-1})(Mx),
\end{equation}
and quantize the conditioned weight $WM^{-1}$ and activation $Mx$. For orthogonal $M$,
$M^{-1}=M^\top$ and error norms are basis-invariant; this subsumes every rotation we study
(ZCA is the lone non-orthogonal case, $M=C^{-1/2}$).

\paragraph{A cheap quantizability proxy.} Closed-loop evaluation is slow; the mechanism is
not. Since the failure mode is outliers eating the per-tensor activation scale, we score a
transform offline by the median INT4 per-tensor activation error after applying $M$,
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{A4err}(x)=\frac{\mathbb{E}|\hat x - x|}{\mathbb{E}|x|},\qquad
\hat x=\mathrm{dequant}^{\text{per-tensor}}_4(Mx),
\end{equation}
from a single $32$-frame calibration pass---ranking all transforms in seconds.

\section{Experiments}
\textbf{Setup.} OpenVLA-OFT policy (bf16 average success $90.25\%$); closed-loop on the four
LIBERO suites \cite{libero}, $10$ tasks $\times\,10$ rollouts $=400$ rollouts per
configuration, $\leq\!600$ steps each. Binomial CI at $400$ rollouts $\approx\pm2.5\%$.

\subsection{Weights are easy; activations are the wall}\label{sec:wall}
Naive weight quantization is near-lossless to 4 bits, but naive W4A4 collapses to $0\%$. A
single rotation restores W4A4 to near-baseline; multiple global rotations cluster at
$88.8$--$90.5\%$ (Table~\ref{tab:main}).

\subsection{Mechanism: rotation crushes activation outliers}\label{sec:mech}
Per backbone linear over a calibration buffer, we measure the activation outlier ratio
(max-channel / median-channel magnitude) and the INT4 per-tensor error, before and after the
rotation (Table~\ref{tab:mech}). Rotation cuts the median outlier ratio $11\times$ and the
worst by four orders of magnitude. The worst single layer
(\texttt{layers.1.mlp.down\_proj}) carries a channel $\sim\!10^5\times$ the median---a
``massive activation''---which alone sets the per-tensor scale and zeroes the rest, explaining
the $0\%$ naive-W4A4 result.

\begin{table}[h]\centering
\caption{Activation conditioning before vs.\ after rotation (median over 224 backbone linears).
Hadamard and random-orthogonal rotations give nearly identical reductions.}
\label{tab:mech}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\toprule
Metric & Pre-rotation & Post-rotation & Factor \\
\midrule
median activation outlier ratio & 18.0 & 1.6 & $11\times$ \\
max activation outlier ratio & 105{,}928 & 6.2 & $17{,}000\times$ \\
median INT4 per-tensor error & 96.5\% & 26.1\% & $3.7\times$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The residual $\sim\!26\%$ is the information-theoretic floor of 4 bits (16 levels) on a
well-conditioned distribution---error halves per added bit (8-bit activations reach
$\sim\!2\%$, i.e.\ lossless). Rotation removes the \emph{outlier} penalty; it cannot remove
the \emph{coarseness} of 4 bits, and getting within $\sim\!1.5$ points of baseline is
therefore near the theoretical limit.

\textbf{Global dense mixers} (1--7) blend every channel into every output $\Rightarrow$ work
($\sim\!27\%$). \textbf{Permutation} merely reorders ($=$ identity). \textbf{Haar} is localized
$\Rightarrow$ cannot spread global outliers. \textbf{Householder/Givens} are partial rotations
(an ``amount-of-mixing'' knob). \textbf{PCA} concentrates energy into top components,
manufacturing a giant outlier---the opposite of what quantization needs. \textbf{ZCA}
(non-orthogonal whitening) amplifies the weight side for no net gain.

\subsection{The proxy predicts closed-loop success}
Closed-loop W4A4 on tier representatives (Table~\ref{tab:val}) shows the proxy correctly
predicts \emph{ordering} (global $\geq$ partial $>$ harmful), though it is \emph{pessimistic
in magnitude} because it uses a per-tensor scale while deployment uses per-token quantization
(PCA: predicted near-$0\%$, observed $50\%$). Use the proxy for triage, closed-loop for
finalists.

full $=$ all suites ($400$).}

\paragraph{Deployment pick: DCT.} Among the tied global mixers, DCT is the practical winner:
unlike Hadamard/SRHT (which fall back to a stored random matrix on the $32$ non-power-of-two
$11{,}008$-dim \texttt{down\_proj} layers), DCT applies the \emph{true} transform on all $224$
backbone linears, is data-free and $O(n\log n)$, and is full-eval-confirmed at $89.8\%$
($99.5\%$ retention).

\subsection{Compression}\label{sec:size}
The checkpoint is $7.69$B parameters ($15.4$\,GB bf16); the quantized backbone is $6.48$B
($84\%$); the tower/heads ($1.22$B, $2.43$\,GB) stay bf16. W4A4 yields $2.7\times$ whole-model
compression ($4\times$ on the backbone); activation bits do not reduce file size but cut
runtime activation bandwidth $\sim\!4\times$ and enable INT4 compute (Table~\ref{tab:size}).

\section{Discussion and Limitations}
Our evaluation is \emph{simulated} quantization: it isolates accuracy but not on-device
latency/energy (Sec.~\ref{sec:device} is the realization). We study one VLA and one benchmark;
generalization to other architectures and physical robots is open. Numbers are single-seed at
$400$ rollouts ($\pm2.5\%$ CI). The proxy predicts ordering, not absolute magnitude; a
per-token variant would tighten it.

\section{Conclusion}
For closed-loop VLA control, activation outliers---not weights---are the quantization
bottleneck, and a single orthogonal rotation removes them, enabling W4A4 at $98$--$100\%$
retention and $2.7\times$ compression. Across 16 transforms, one principle predicts
everything---spread energy, do not concentrate it; global mixers tie at $\sim\!90\%$, DCT is
the deployment pick, and PCA is a cautionary tale. A seconds-long proxy reproduces the
ranking. We release the code, harness, and leaderboard so the community can build on, rather
than re-run, this study.
